Overview

What is Simon Smart?

Simon Smart is an intuitive smart home application designed for relaxed home automation experiences. The design bridges digital interfaces with traditional physical operations, significantly shortening users' learning curve.

Preconfigured and customizable scenes based on daily routines are presented through animated illustrations, creating intimate and joyful interactions.

My Role

Product Design Intern

Redesigned Simon Smart's interface to prioritize scene-based automation over individual device controls. Transformed the app from device-focused to scenario-driven experience.

Created prominent scene cards, streamlined device management, and improved user flows for intuitive everyday home automation.

The Outcome

The Scene-First Interface

Scene-based controls allow users to manage multiple devices simultaneously through preset automation sequences like Rise, Leave, Return, and Sleep. Over four months, I was the lead designer on this transformation, where I designed a more intuitive, scenario-driven interface that accommodates both automated lifestyle routines and detailed device customization.

Lighting Control Demo

Problem Framing

The problem and vision

Simon Smart previously operated with a device-first approach, requiring users to manually control each smart device individually. This created friction in daily routines where users needed to adjust multiple devices for common scenarios.

The challenge was to transform the user experience from device-centric to scenario-driven, making smart home automation feel natural and effortless for everyday use.

Investigate base on the Problem

XIAOMI MIUI HOME - DETAILED ANALYSIS

To validate our approach and understand industry patterns, I analyzed leading smart home platforms including Xiaomi MIUI HOME. This research revealed that successful smart home interfaces prioritize quick access to commonly used functions while maintaining comprehensive device control options.

Analysis Findings

4 out of 5 competitors maintained device-oriented layouts, forcing users through equipment navigation. Only Huawei approached scene-orientation, but still required room-by-room device understanding.

Validation

Despite users performing the same 3-5 scenarios 80% of the time, no platform prioritized scene-based automation, validating Simon Smart's differentiation opportunity.

Impact & Results

The transformation from a device-first to scene-first approach significantly improved user engagement and satisfaction. The new interface made smart home automation more accessible to users of all technical backgrounds, creating a more intuitive and enjoyable home automation experience.

The design successfully bridged the gap between digital innovation and traditional home interaction patterns, making advanced technology feel familiar and approachable.
